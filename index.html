Blog:

On the Culture series

I know I'm a decade or two late, but lately I've been hooked on Iain M. Banks's science-fiction series about The Culture. If you haven't heard of it, The Culture is a series of books set in a fictional post-scarcity, spacefaring society. All of its citizens are perfectly healthy, live for hundreds of years (if they choose to die at all), and have no worries, as everything is run by hyper-intelligent artificial Minds. Of course this makes for boring stories, so most of the stories in this world concern people on the periphery of this Culture.

Reading it, I'm torn whether or not this is a good Utopia to live in. Of course, to live without war, disease, or even minor discomfort is something that anybody should want. It's probably a sign of my relative comfort that I'm able to even ponder this; a person who right now has nothing to eat would certainly think I'm crazy for not wanting to spend life in paradise. At the same time, though, I think there is something missing from Utopian experience. All the pleasure in the universe is great, but I think in this world I would feel unfulfilled. There's no reason to do anything! This isn't something anyone on Earth is going to have to think about for a while, but it is still food for thought, and an example of my favorite kind of science fiction: the kind that makes you re-examine something about humanity today.
